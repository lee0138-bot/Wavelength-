Understanding emotions

What are you
going through?

Every feeling has a name, and every feeling makes sense. Here's what might be happening — and what can help.

💭

Anxiety

That constant worry, racing thoughts, or tight chest — anxiety is your nervous system working overtime. It's incredibly common in teens, and there are real tools to manage it.

🌧️

Sadness & Depression

Feeling empty, low, or like nothing matters can be signs of depression. You're not broken — your brain chemistry needs support, just like a body needs treatment when it's sick.

📚

Stress & Burnout

School, expectations, social pressure — it's a lot. Chronic stress is real and it affects your sleep, concentration, and mood. You deserve breaks, and asking for help is smart.

🔥

Anger

Anger is a signal, not a flaw. Often it's covering hurt, fear, or frustration. Learning to understand and channel your anger — rather than suppress it — is a real strength.

🌿

Loneliness

You can feel alone in a crowded room. Social media makes it worse by making everyone else's life look perfect. You're not the only one who feels disconnected — truly.

🌊

Overwhelm

When everything feels like too much at once, your brain goes into survival mode. Learning to pause, breathe, and take things one step at a time is a skill that can be practised.

Anxiety grounding technique

The 5-4-3-2-1
method

When anxiety spikes, bring yourself back to the present. Type out your responses and check them off as you find them.

5

Things you can see

Look around and notice five distinct physical items. Pay attention to light or shadows.

4

Things you can touch

Acknowledge four textures surrounding you (clothing, a phone screen, cold metal, wood).

3

Things you can hear

Listen intently for three ambient noises (clock ticking, traffic far away, wind whistling).

2

Things you can smell

Identify two smells. If there are none near, imagine your favorite crisp morning air or soap scent.

1

Thing you can taste

Focus on one flavor remaining on your tongue, or step away for a slow sip of cold water.

0 / 5

Checking off these boxes systematically activates the analytical prefrontal cortex of your brain, signaling your primitive amygdala to down-regulate its fight-or-flight distress code.
