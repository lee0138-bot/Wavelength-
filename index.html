Emotions Decoded

Understanding what you're going through

Name what you're feeling to help take away its power. Here's a quick guide to common emotions.

🔮

Anxiety

A racing heart, looping "what-ifs," or a tight feeling in your chest. It's your brain trying too hard to protect you from future threats.

🌧️

Sadness

A heavy, low-energy sensation. It can make you want to pull away or cry. It's a natural signal that you are processing a hurt or a loss.

⚡

Stress

Feeling physically pressured, overwhelmed by demands, or scattered. It happens when challenges feel larger than our current energy levels.

🔥

Anger

A hot explosion of energy, clenching fists, or frustration. Often acts like a shield protecting softer, hidden feelings like hurt or unfairness.

🪐

Loneliness

An empty or hollow ache that tells you you are disconnected or unseen. It's a natural signal driving us toward meaningful human connection.

🌊

Overwhelm

A flooding sensation where everything hits at once, making it impossible to focus. It means your sensory or cognitive cup is completely full.

Anxiety Circuit-Breaker

The 5-4-3-2-1 Grounding Technique

When thoughts spin, re-engage your physical senses to find stability in the absolute present room.

5

Things you can SEE around you

Look around and notice small details: a shadow, a plant pattern, a reflection.

4

Things you can FEEL physically

Notice physical anchors: fabric textures, cold air, your back against the chair.

3

Things you can HEAR clearly

Listen past your internal noise: a distant car hum, clock tick, wind blowing.

2

Things you can SMELL right now

Inhale slowly: notice clothing soap, wood furniture, or just the ambient clean air.

1

Thing you can TASTE or say out loud

Notice the state of your palette, or say a simple, real fact: "I am safe right now."

5

Steps remaining to establish presence. Focus on your surroundings one step at a time.

Immediate Support Networks

You never have to navigate alone

If you feel completely overwhelmed, unsafe, or simply need an objective, trained human to speak with, reach out to these entirely free, secure, and fully confidential resources 24/7:

Mental Health Crisis

988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline

National support network offering entirely free, highly secure, confidential emotional relief chat or call lines.

Call or Text 988
Crisis Text Line

Crisis Text Support

Connect immediately with a live, expert crisis counselor for any heavy mental pressure over instant SMS.

Text HOME to 741741
LGBTQ+ Affirming

The Trevor Project

Safe, supportive, protective counselors specific to answering LGBTQ youth experiencing depression, anxiety, or fear.

Call 1-866-488-7386
Teen Peer Support

Teen Line

Talk directly with specialized trained teens who completely empathize and walk through youth problems together.

Call 1-800-852-8336
Child Advocacy

Childhelp National Helpline

Dedicated completely to child safety and abuse prevention, offering supportive professional intervention advisors.

Call or Text 1-800-422-4453
Substance Support

SAMHSA Lifeline

Treatment referral routing information service supporting mental health concerns or substance distress.

Call 1-800-662-4357
Domestic Safety

Domestic Violence Helpline

Highly secure routing link ensuring domestic relationship safety planning tools and advocacy validation lines.

Call 1-800-799-7233
Assault Support

RAINN Network

The largest secure nationwide network addressing physical safety trauma links and direct resource validation lines.

Call 1-800-656-4673

Resources and direct treatment referral support maps tailored for individuals fighting eating disorder and body image issues.
